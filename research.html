Research Interests

Combining experimental measurements with multi-level modeling techniques, such as Finite Element Method, Phase Field, Molecular Dynamics Simulation and First Principle Calculation, understand the nano and micro-mechanics of materials at the atomic level, and guide the design and synthesis of advanced materials.

 

Feature Research

(a) Detailed views of the peeling front and edges, where the width w changes with peeling. The results are obtained from our coarse-grained molecular dynamics simulations. (b) The feasibility factor σs/γi calculated for two-dimensional sheets on the substrate. (Gao E et al. J. Mech. Phys. Solids, 115, 248-262, 2018) (c) Tensile stiffness K, bending stiffness D. (d) The factor of consistency, c, calculated for two-dimensional materials, showing the difference in the definitions of thickness of two-dimensional materials—the thin-shell thickness or interlayer distance in their layer-by-layer assemblies. (Gao E. et al. J. Appl. Mech. 82, 121012, 2015)

 

The strength reduction from individual, defect-free carbon nanotubes to carbon nanotube fibers, brokendown into a three-level hierarchy.(Gao E. et al. Carbon 138, 134-142 , 2018)
